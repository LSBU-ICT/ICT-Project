what are my rights?

    Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is considered "faulty good”?

    • The following condition will provide you legal right to claim for the “faulty good” under the Consumer Rights Act 2015.
      1. Not to satisfactory, Section 9
      2. Not fit for purpose, Section 10
      3. Not as described, Section 11

  • What rights do I have if the good is considered faulty?

    • Following the Consumer Rights Act 2015, you will have the right to refund, repair or replacement for “faulty good”.

  • What is my consumer’s right to refund?

    • For Digital Content
     - Consumer has 14 days limited to exercise the right to a full refund. Subject to the fact the item has not begun to or downloaded with acknowledgement.
     - If the goods are faulty, the fault can’t be fixed, or if it hasn’t been fixed within a reasonable time and without significant inconvenience, the consumer is entitled to some or a full refund.

    For Goods ordered online
     - Consumer has 14 days limited to exercise the right to a full refund.
     - Only when the goods are faulty, consumer has 30 days limited to exercise the right to a full refund.

    For Goods bought in Store
     - Only when the goods are faulty, consumer has 30 days limited to exercise the right to a full refund.

  • What is the time limit to claim for repair or replacement?

    • You have a 6 months’ time limit to make a claim for the “faulty good”.

  • What if the good did not last a reasonable length of time?

    • You have a 6 years’ time limit to make a claim for refund, repair or replacement.

  • What if the repair or replacement is still faulty?

    • You can ask for a discount on the original price and keep the repaired or replaced good. Or you can ask for the full amount back if it had less than 6 months since you bought the good.

  • What to say to the store owner (applicable to both online and high street shop)?

    • “Since [the good you purchased] is [faulty / doesn't match the description] my rights have been breach, therefore, I would like a [repair / replacement / refund].”
    Or
    For Digital Content and Goods ordered online
    “Following Consumer Contracts Regulations, I have 14 days right to a full refund.”
    To see free sample complaint letter please see [ letters ]

  • What can I do if the store is not replying?

    •  1. Make a formal complaint.
     2. Contact the trading association the store is a member of.
     3. Contact the Payment provider (i.e., Visa, PayPal);
     4. Consider Alternative Dispute Resolution.
     5. Make a court claim.

  • What is Alternative Dispute Resolution

    • Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) is a process that enables disputes between a consumer and a business entity or seller to be settled a dispute by an independent mechanism outside the court system.

    In general, the parties will attempt to resolve the dispute between themselves, through the business’ own complaints procedure. However, in failure to resolve a dispute ADR gives the parties another option rather the taking the dispute through the court system.

    Moreover, since some ADR mechanisms’ decision can be enforced through the court or appealed to the court. It can be cheaper and quicker for parties rather than taking a dispute through the court system.

    There are different forms of ADR:

     - Conciliation
     - Mediation
     - Arbitration
